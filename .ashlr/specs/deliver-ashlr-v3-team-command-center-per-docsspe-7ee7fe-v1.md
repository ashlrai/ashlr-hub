## Context
Ashlr is building a secure, decentralized command center for teams to manage tasks remotely using its software-as-a-service platform ashlr v3 Team Command Center (TCC). The initiative aims at enhancing collaboration among distributed workforce members by providing robust security features and seamless team management tools.

**Who:** Ashlr development & engineering team
**For Whom:** Remote work-oriented businesses, decentralized teams that require secure communication channels.
**What Problem Solves:** Improves the efficiency of task execution across geographically dispersed workers while ensuring data privacy through encrypted communications. Reduces overhead for managing multiple connections and identities by unifying them under a single platform.

## North Star
Achieve full operational capability with ashlr v3 TCC, enabling teams to securely manage tasks from any location without sacrificing security or performance metrics like low latency and high throughput in communication channels over the API.ais (one team memory).

## Operating Principles

- Safety-first: Prioritize user data privacy through end-to-end encryption.
- Scalability: Ensure that new features can be seamlessly integrated for increased load as teams grow.

## Pillars
1. **Secure Collaboration** - Establish a secure, decentralized environment where tasks and communications are encrypted using advanced cryptographic protocols to protect against unauthorized access or breaches of sensitive information while maintaining efficient collaboration among team members.
2. **Identity Management & Attribution** - Implement robust identity management systems that attribute actions correctly across distributed teams ensuring accountability without compromising privacy through pro-token identities integrated with the command center's features.

3. **Intelligent Task Execution and Memory Syncing:** Develop intelligent task execution platforms where tasks are not only assigned based on expertise but also team memory is synchronized over an API enabling seamless transition of work processes among different devices, eliminating inefficiency in manual handoffs across teams spread globally.
4. **Robust Data Management** - Establish mechanisms for creating new accounts while maintaining a consistent state between the server and clients to ensure that all data related to tasks performed by multiple members are stored securely on both servers as well as client-side ensuring seamless collaboration with no loss of information, even when team members switch devices.

## Roadmap
### Phase 1: Foundations - Initial Setup & Role Management

**Goal:** Lay down the foundation for secure command center setup and role management.
- Import specifications (spec import) to manage different tasks effectively in ashlr v3 TCC based on varying roles within a project/team environment.
- Implement actor attribution mechanisms that ensure correct identification of users across distributed platforms using pro-token identities.

**Deliverables:**
1. Propose new methods for importing and managing specification documents tailored specifically toward team command center applications (spec import).
2. Create an initial role management framework enabling teams to assign specific tasks based on unique roles effectively while maintaining the confidentiality & security requirements associated with sensitive project data.
3. Establish a secure method of attributor identity assignment across distributed platforms leveraging pro-token identities.

### Phase 2: Seamless Team Genome Sync and Shared Inbox

**Goal:** Develop an encrypted shared inbox that can seamlessly sync team genome over ashlr v3 TCC while ensuring complete end-to-end encryption (E2EE) for all communications.
- Create mechanisms to enable seamless transfer of knowledge, task assignments & progress updates through the team's command center across different devices and locations.

**Deliverables:**
1. Develop a shared inbox that can securely sync team genome over ashlr v3 TCC while maintaining end-to-end encryption (E2EE) for all communications.
2. Implement mechanisms to establish an encrypted connection between members of distributed teams through the platform, allowing them access on their own devices and facilitating seamless task assignments across varying locations.

### Phase 3: Intelligent Task Execution & Robust Data Management

**Goal:** Develop a system where tasks are assigned intelligently based upon expertise within team members while ensuring robust data management mechanisms for consistent memory states.
- Establish systems to handle new accounts seamlessly, integrating them into the existing command center setup and maintaining uniformity across server-client interactions.

**Deliverables:**
1. Intelligent task execution platform leveraging pro-token identities that assigns work efficiently among distributed teams based on expertise levels within team members while ensuring consistency between servers & clients with regard for information security.
2. Develop mechanisms to handle new accounts seamlessly, integrating them into the existing command center setup and maintaining uniformity across server-client interactions.

### Phase 4: Remote Kill Capability

**Goal:** Implement a remote kill capability mechanism that allows teams access over ashlr v3 TCC ensuring complete control even during emergencies or unexpected circumstances such as malicious attacks.
- Develop an efficient method of implementing the desired functionality in scenarios where team members need to disconnect from work remotely for emergency purposes without compromising security.

**Deliverables:**
1. Implement a remote kill mechanism that allows teams access over ashlr v3 TCC ensuring complete control even during emergencies or unexpected circumstances such as malicious attacks.
2. Develop mechanisms allowing users the option of executing secure connections through their own devices via distributed systems leveraging intelligent task management capabilities for effective collaboration among team members.
